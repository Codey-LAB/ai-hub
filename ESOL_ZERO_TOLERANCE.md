## PART II — LEGAL FRAMEWORK (DEUTSCHES RECHT / GERMAN LAW)

The following provisions of German criminal and civil law apply directly to the prohibited activities enumerated in Part I. This addendum explicitly invokes these statutes to provide **legal teeth** beyond the scope of a standard software license.

> **Note:** This addendum is governed by the laws of Germany (Berlin), consistent with ESOL v1.1, Section 8.

---

### § 202a StGB — Ausspähen von Daten
*(Unauthorized Access to Data)*

Applies to: Section 1.2, 3.2
Unauthorized access to data that is specially protected against access — including credential harvesting, session hijacking, and mass-scanning of protected systems — constitutes a criminal offense punishable by **imprisonment up to 3 years** or a fine.

---

### § 202b StGB — Abfangen von Daten
*(Interception of Data)*

Applies to: Section 1.2
The unauthorized interception of non-public data transmissions (e.g., session tokens, credentials via MitM attacks) is a criminal offense punishable by **imprisonment up to 2 years** or a fine.

---

### § 202c StGB — Vorbereiten des Ausspähens und Abfangens von Daten
*(Hacker-Paragraph — Preparation of Data Espionage)*

Applies to: Section 1.1, 1.3, 3.1, 3.2
The creation, distribution, or possession of tools intended for unauthorized data access — including the "Fake GPT/LLM Wrappers" and "Mass-Scanning" tools described in Part I — constitutes criminal preparation, punishable by **imprisonment up to 2 years** or a fine.

> This paragraph explicitly covers the distribution of "hacking tools" as a service or product.

---

### § 263 StGB — Betrug
*(Fraud)*

Applies to: Section 2.1, 2.2, 2.3, 4.1
Deliberate deception causing financial harm to another person — including fake "VIP hacking channels," fraudulent crypto schemes, and recovery scams — constitutes fraud, punishable by **imprisonment up to 5 years** or a fine. Aggravated fraud (§ 263 Abs. 3) may result in **imprisonment of 6 months to 10 years**.

---

### § 263a StGB — Computerbetrug
*(Computer Fraud)*

Applies to: Section 1.2, 1.3, 4.1
Manipulation of data processing systems to cause financial damage — including credential draining, wallet attacks, and automated phishing campaigns — constitutes computer fraud, punishable by **imprisonment up to 5 years** or a fine.

---

### § 269 StGB — Fälschung beweiserheblicher Daten
*(Falsification of Legally Relevant Data)*

Applies to: Section 3.3, 4.2
The manipulation or fabrication of data intended to serve as legal evidence — including fake CVE reports, falsified bug bounty findings, and fabricated "proof" of system access used for extortion — is punishable by **imprisonment up to 5 years** or a fine.

---

### § 303a StGB — Datenveränderung
*(Data Manipulation)*

Applies to: Section 1.2, 3.2
The unauthorized deletion, suppression, rendering unusable, or modification of data constitutes a criminal offense punishable by **imprisonment up to 2 years** or a fine.

---

### § 303b StGB — Computersabotage
*(Computer Sabotage)*

Applies to: Section 3.2
Interference with data processing systems of substantial significance — including DoS/DDoS attacks and unauthorized infrastructure disruption — is punishable by **imprisonment up to 3 years** or a fine. If the affected infrastructure is of critical importance, penalties increase to **imprisonment up to 10 years**.

---

### § 238 StGB — Nachstellung
*(Stalking / Cyberstalking)*

Applies to: Section 4.2
Persistent harassment, stalking, or psychological manipulation of individuals using digital tools — including the use of fabricated technical "evidence" to intimidate — constitutes a criminal offense punishable by **imprisonment up to 3 years** or a fine.

---

### § 253 StGB — Erpressung
*(Extortion)*

Applies to: Section 4.1, 4.2
Coercing individuals into payments or actions through threats or the simulation of unauthorized system access constitutes extortion, punishable by **imprisonment up to 5 years** or a fine. Aggravated extortion may result in **imprisonment of 1 to 10 years**.

---

### UWG — Gesetz gegen den unlauteren Wettbewerb
*(Act Against Unfair Competition)*

Applies to: Section 2.2, 4.3
Misleading commercial practices — including the fraudulent marketing of software capabilities, fake technical authority, and open-source parasitism — constitute unfair competition under the UWG, actionable through **civil injunctions and damages claims**.

---

### DSGVO — Datenschutz-Grundverordnung (Art. 83)
*(GDPR — General Data Protection Regulation)*

Applies to: Section 1.2, 1.3, 3.2
Unauthorized collection, processing, or exfiltration of personal data violates the GDPR. Penalties for serious violations can reach **€20,000,000 or 4% of global annual turnover**, whichever is higher.
